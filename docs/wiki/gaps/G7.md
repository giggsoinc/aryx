## What Was Built

`resolution/confidence.py` (DEC-006): confidence = **minimum merge-edge
score** on the cluster — a chain is as trustworthy as its weakest merge.
Min, not mean: one 0.61 edge can't hide behind three 0.95s.

- Singletons: 0.5 (uncontested, also unconfirmed — honest prior)
- Human-approved edges: 0.99 (reviewed beats inferred, never claims 1.0)
- Clamp [0.5, 0.99] — certainty isn't on offer
- Wired into `_materialize`, which BOTH resolution paths share (legacy
  `resolve()` and `resolve_chunked`) — replaces the old binary
  `1.0 if len > 1 else 0.5`.

Merging logic untouched — asserted by test (cluster sets identical) and by
`make er-bench-quick`: P=1.00 R=0.59 F1=0.74, byte-identical to baseline.

**Marketing line:** every entity now carries an auditable confidence derived
from its weakest merge decision.
