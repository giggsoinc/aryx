2The seven CX/HITL principles every screen obeys

1

Propose, don't impose

Everything the AI does is a reversible proposal — ghost state, one-click accept / edit / reject. Nothing is silently applied.

Map: dashed nodes · R-2 one-click dismiss
2

Never approve blind

Every proposal carries a confidence score and provenance to the source. The human decides with evidence, not faith.

T-2 provenance · T-0d verified citation
3

Surgical attention

Auto-handle the routine; surface only the outliers, low-confidence, and governance-sensitive. Respect the human's time.

A-2′ deliberation on ~1% · band routing
4

Progressive disclosure

A first-timer sees plain language and the map; an expert drills to OWL, axioms, SKOS. Same product, two depths.

R-1 plain-language alias · R-3 badges on hover
5

The loop compounds

Every approval is labeled data. The engine gets better per domain; the human does less work over time. Stickiness by value.

Engine 6 · HITL learning loop
6

Trust by transparency

Audit trail, deliberation rationale, the Accuracy Lab. Magnus and Marcus trust because they can SEE — not because we promise.

T-4 audit · A-3 recorded rationale
7

Zero-to-hero, never labeled

A user who knows nothing about ontology is guided to a defensible model — and never called a novice. Designed for the clueless, said to no one.

T-1 novice → governed
