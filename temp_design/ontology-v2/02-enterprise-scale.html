The ontology is small even when the data is huge.

200M rows · 50 tables · 5 Salesforce orgs · 1TB Google Drive · ServiceNow. This page is how Aryx survives that — and how the product (not a pitch deck) satisfies the CFO, the CDO, and the Palantir skeptic at once.

1The architectural insight that beats scale

Palantir's pipeline is incremental and schema-first under the hood. So is ours — that's why it's both cheap and fast. You never LLM 200M rows, and you never render them.

The data — huge
200,000,000

rows of instances. Streamed, sampled for classification, resolved via chunked blocking, projected incrementally. Never all-LLM'd. Never all-rendered.

The ontology — small
~50–200

classes + relations + properties. Bounded by the schema, not the row count. This is what the Map shows, what the agent reasons over, what you govern. It barely grows when rows hit a billion.

Why this is the whole game: classification cost scales with schema size, not row count — so the CFO's bill is flat as data explodes. The Map renders the small ontology, not the huge instance graph. Resolution + sync are incremental. That single insight answers scale, cost, AND interactivity at once.
